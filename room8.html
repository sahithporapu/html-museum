Non-semantic tags are HTML elements that do not describe their meaning to the browser or the developer. These tags are used mainly for layout and styling, but their purpose is not clear just by looking at them.

They tell the browser "what to show" but not "what it means". You often need to use classes or IDs to explain their purpose.

Tag Description
<div> Division tag a container used to group content
<span> Inline container used to style parts of text

ðŸ†š Semantic vs Non-Semantic

  • Semantic: <header>, <footer>, <nav> tells the browser what the content is
  • Non-Semantic: <div>, <span> tells the browser how to group, not what it means

ðŸ“Œ Example:

Non-semantic:

  <div class="title">Welcome to the HTML Museum</div>
  <div class="content">This is where the content goes.</div>
</div>

Without reading the classes or IDs, we don't know what these divs are for. That's why semantic tags are preferred today.
